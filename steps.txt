Feature Selection

"We are using a Hybrid Feature Selection Strategy we apply LightGBM Feature Importance to rank the remaining variables by their 'Information Gain.' This ensures our model stays 'Lean'—focusing only on high-signal features like $P_2$ while ignoring low-impact noise."

As our data is imbalanced so we use stratified sampling to sample or measure data

"We chose to perform feature selection on our Aggregated Profile Dataset rather than the raw transactional rows. This allowed us to evaluate the importance of temporal features—such as the difference between a customer's 'Mean' and 'Last' balance—which are the strongest indicators of a sudden shift in credit risk." 

Teacher: "Doesn't changing the balance of the data mess up the probability scores?"
Your Answer: "Yes, for the final model, it would. But for Feature Selection, we only care about the Ranking of the features. A balanced dataset is actually better for finding which features are the best 'separators'

"We actually implemented a Stochastic Stability Selection approach, which is a specialized form of Bagging. By training our LightGBM selector across multiple stratified sub-samples and averaging the Information Gain, we reduced the variance of our feature rankings. This gave us the stability of Bagging with the high-precision feature interaction detection of Gradient Boosting."
